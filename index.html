Will Allen

Urban Farmer and Community Activist

Highlights of Allen's life:

  • In the late 1960's, Allen was the first african-american basketball player at University of Miami, Fl
  • Drafted by NBA but ended up playing professionally in Belgium.
  • retired from professional basketball in 1978 and went to work for Proctor and Gamble
  • In 1993, he left P&G and purchased Growing Power
  • Growing Power is the last working farm inside the Milwaukee City Limits.
  • Growing Power is an urban farm providing fresh food to communities without nearby access to it (often refered to as a food desert).
  • Allen is a cancer survivor.
  • He has received a Ford Foundation Leadership Grant, a Macarthur Genious Grant, and Kellog Foundation Grant for his work associated with Growing Power.
  • Allen co-authored the book The Good Food Revolution: Growing Healthy Food, People and Communities with Charles Wilson. The book is the story of Allen's life and the events leading up to the purchase of Growing Power and the trials and tribulations of turning it into a working farm that benefitted the surrounding community.
  • Today, Growing Power conducts numerous workshops and demonstrations on agriculture, horticulture, and agriculture.
  • It manages a series of farms around Milwaukee, WI, and Chicago, IL.
  • You can learn more about Growing Power at their website.
